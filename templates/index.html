About MOOQ

Classification Features

pH

Optimal milk pH helps maintain microbiological stability and extends shelf life. A good pH in milk generally ranges from 6.5 to 6.7.

Temperature

A temperature range of around 0-4 degrees Celsius (32-39 degrees Fahrenheit) helps maintain milk freshness and prevents the growth of unwanted bacteria.

Odor

A good odor in milk should be fresh and neutral. Good milk should not have a strong or unusual odor.


Taste

High-quality milk should have a delicious and pleasant taste, without any noticeable sour or bitter taste.

Fat

Healthy fresh milk has natural fat content, which provides the desired flavor and creaminess.

Turbidity

High levels of turbidity can be an indication of contamination or fouling, which can affect the quality of the milk


Color

Good fresh milk has a clean, clear white color, indicating its quality and cleanliness.
